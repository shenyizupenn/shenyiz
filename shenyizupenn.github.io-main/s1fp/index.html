Let's Go!

The project "Ecological Coexistence: Permaflow Gardens" embodies a visionary integration of architecture, agriculture, and nature, inspired by the intricate symbiosis observed in treehouses and Pixel Farming. This initiative targets both local farmers and tourists in Ojai, aiming to offer them a novel farming experience grounded in organic practices, ecological mindfulness, and experiential living. The project's ethos is to transcend mere cohabitation of species, creating a vibrant, productive ecosystem that fosters a deep connection between humans and nature.
At the heart of this endeavor is a commitment to organic farming and the study of local plant species and innovative farming techniques like pixel farming. Through onsite visits and extensive research, the project seeks to develop a farming system that harmonizes with the local environment, benefiting both the ecosystem and the community.
The design process begins with an exploration of geometric patterns, embracing circles, curves, and tangent lines to discover new forms and spatial arrangements. This foundational work emphasizes scale, direction, and movement, aiming to create dynamic, engaging spaces that inspire and intrigue. By pushing the boundaries of form and color, the project explores the interplay of positive and negative space, seeking to create a visually and experientially rich environment.
As the project evolves, these initial patterns and shapes are reorganized and layered to define various zones within the farm, including static, dynamic, and flexible areas. This rule-based system of design leads to the creation of a diversified architectural landscape that is both functional and aesthetically pleasing, drawing color inspiration from artists like Sam Gilliam and Laura Owens to enrich the visual experience.
